Assistant in Research

I started working as an Assistant in Research for University of Michigan EECS Professor John E. Laird in January 2019. I have contributed to several exciting projects thus far.

Reducing Global Cumulative Odometry Error with Overhead Camera Localization

I led a group of two other undergraduate students for this project. We designed, built, and programmed an overhead camera system for localization and reducing global cumulative odometry error in robot mapping using ArUco fiducials from the OpenCV computer vision library, linear transformations and change of basis, and multithreaded Python programming.

Keywords: localization, linear algebra, computer vision, odometry, robot mapping

Status: seeking opportunity to publish

Reinforcement Learning

We are integrating the Soar Cognitive Architecture with Anki's Cozmo robots. We are writing Soar production code and some Python programs to control Cozmo. One of the goals of the project is to use Reinforcement Learning in Soar to train intelligent agents to complete simple tasks such as stacking cubes and exploring unknown environments with rewards.

Status: in progress

Graphical Representation of Soar

We are building a high-level physical representation of Soar to help illustrate the architecture. The end product can be a drag and drop programming language GUI of Soar. It can integrate with the Cozmo robots and enable us to teach AI with fun toys.

Status: future work
